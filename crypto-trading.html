Crypto Markets Overview

Always-On Markets

  • 24/7/365 — no market close, no holidays
  • Global participation across every timezone
  • Fragmented liquidity across 500+ exchanges
  • Price discrepancies create arbitrage opportunities
  • Weekend volatility spikes are common

Market Characteristics

  • High volatility — 5-10% daily swings are normal
  • BTC dominance drives altcoin correlation
  • Macro events (Fed, regulations) cause rapid moves
  • Thin liquidity in altcoins = large slippage
  • Market cap: ~$2-3T (vs $50T+ equities)

Exchanges & Trading Pairs

CEX (Centralized)

  • Binance, Bybit, OKX, Coinbase
  • Custodial — exchange holds your funds
  • High liquidity, fast execution
  • KYC/AML requirements
  • Maker fees: 0.01-0.1%, Taker: 0.02-0.1%

DEX (Decentralized)

  • Uniswap, dYdX, Jupiter, Raydium
  • Non-custodial — you hold your keys
  • AMM-based or order book (dYdX)
  • No KYC, permissionless access
  • Gas fees + swap fees (0.3% typical)

Pair notation: BTC/USDT = buy/sell BTC priced in USDT. Base asset / Quote asset.

Spot Trading

  • Direct ownership — buy and sell the actual cryptocurrency
  • Settlement is immediate (or T+0 on most exchanges)
  • Spot price = current market price for instant delivery
  • No leverage, no liquidation risk — you own the asset
  • Can withdraw to personal wallet for self-custody
  • Profit only when price goes up (no native shorting)

Perpetual Futures

How They Work

  • No expiry date — hold indefinitely
  • Trade with leverage (1x to 125x)
  • Long (profit from up) or Short (profit from down)
  • Mark price vs Last price for liquidation
  • Funding rate anchors perp price to spot

Margin & Liquidation

  • Initial margin = collateral to open position
  • Maintenance margin = minimum to stay open
  • Cross margin: shares balance across positions
  • Isolated margin: limits loss to position margin
  • Liquidation = forced closure when margin exhausted
# 10x Long: $1,000 margin controls $10,000 position
# BTC at $64,000 → buy 0.15625 BTC worth of contracts
# If BTC rises 5% → profit = $500 (50% return on margin)
# If BTC falls 10% → liquidated (100% loss of margin)
# Liquidation price ≈ entry * (1 - 1/leverage) = $64,000 * 0.9 = $57,600

Funding Rates

  • Purpose: keep perpetual futures price aligned with spot price
  • Payments exchanged between longs and shorts every 8 hours
  • Settlement times: 00:00, 08:00, 16:00 UTC (most exchanges)
  • Positive rate — longs pay shorts (market is bullish)
  • Negative rate — shorts pay longs (market is bearish)
  • Typical range: -0.01% to +0.03% per 8h period

Calculation

Funding = Position Size × Funding Rate

$10,000 × 0.01% = $1 per 8h

Annualized Cost

0.01% × 3/day × 365 = 10.95%/year

Can exceed lending rates significantly

Cash-and-Carry Arbitrage

Long spot + short perp = collect funding while delta-neutral. Low risk, steady yield.

Sentiment Signal

Extreme positive funding = over-leveraged longs. Often precedes liquidation cascades.

Technical Indicators

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

Momentum oscillator (0-100). Overbought >70, oversold <30. Period: 14 candles typical.

MACD

Trend-following: EMA(12) - EMA(26) vs signal line EMA(9). Crossovers signal momentum shifts.

Bollinger Bands

SMA(20) ± 2σ. Price touching upper/lower band signals potential reversal or breakout.

ATR (Average True Range)

Volatility measure. Use for dynamic stop-loss sizing: stop = entry - 2×ATR. Period: 14.

EMA (Exponential Moving Average)

Weighted toward recent prices. EMA(9)/EMA(21) crossover for trend direction. Faster than SMA.

VWAP (Volume-Weighted Avg Price)

Fair value benchmark. Price above VWAP = bullish bias. Institutional execution benchmark.

# Calculate RSI with pandas
import pandas as pd
delta = df['close'].diff()
gain = delta.where(delta > 0, 0).rolling(14).mean()
loss = (-delta.where(delta < 0, 0)).rolling(14).mean()
rs = gain / loss
df['rsi'] = 100 - (100 / (1 + rs))

On-Chain Analysis

Whale Tracking

  • Monitor wallets holding >1,000 BTC
  • Large transfers to exchanges = potential selling pressure
  • Accumulation patterns signal conviction
  • Tools: Whale Alert, Arkham Intelligence, Nansen

DEX/CEX Volume Ratios

  • Rising DEX volume = growing DeFi activity
  • CEX outflows = users moving to self-custody (bullish)
  • CEX inflows = potential sell pressure
  • Stablecoin flows indicate capital rotation

NVT Ratio (Network Value to Transactions)

Market cap / daily transaction volume. High NVT = overvalued network. Crypto's P/E ratio.

Exchange Reserves

Total BTC held on exchanges. Declining reserves = supply shock potential. Track via CryptoQuant.

  • Order book — list of all open buy/sell orders at each price level
  • Bid-ask spread — difference between best bid and best ask (tighter = more liquid)
  • Depth — total volume at each price level; thin depth = easy to move price
  • Slippage — difference between expected and actual fill price
  • Market impact — how your order moves the price; larger orders = more impact
  • Iceberg orders — large orders hidden in small visible chunks

Position Sizing & Risk Management

Percent Risk Per Trade

  • Risk 1-2% of total portfolio per trade
  • Position = (Portfolio × Risk%) / (Entry - StopLoss)
  • Example: $100K portfolio, 1% risk, $200 stop
  • Position = $1,000 / $200 = 5 units
  • Survive 10+ consecutive losers

Advanced Controls

  • Kelly Criterion — optimal bet sizing: f* = (bp - q) / b
  • Max drawdown limit — halt trading at -15% to -20%
  • Daily loss limit — stop after -3% in a single day
  • Correlation check — avoid 5 correlated BTC longs
  • Half-Kelly — use 50% of Kelly for safety margin
# Position sizing with ATR-based stop
atr = df['atr_14'].iloc[-1]          # e.g., $1,200
stop_distance = 2 * atr               # $2,400
risk_amount = portfolio * 0.01         # $1,000 on $100K portfolio
position_size = risk_amount / stop_distance  # 0.4167 BTC

Backtesting & Walk-Forward Testing

Backtesting Essentials

  • Simulate strategy on historical data
  • Look-ahead bias — never use future data in signals
  • Survivorship bias — include delisted tokens
  • Model realistic fees, slippage, funding costs
  • Test across bull, bear, and sideways markets

Walk-Forward Validation

  • Train on window 1, test on window 2
  • Slide window forward, repeat
  • Prevents overfitting to a single period
  • Out-of-sample Sharpe > 1.5 is promising
  • Paper trade for 2-4 weeks before going live
# Walk-forward backtest structure
for train_start, train_end, test_start, test_end in windows:
    model.fit(data[train_start:train_end])
    signals = model.predict(data[test_start:test_end])
    pnl = simulate_trades(signals, fees=0.0006, slippage=0.0002)
    results.append({'sharpe': pnl.sharpe(), 'max_dd': pnl.max_drawdown()})

avg_sharpe = np.mean([r['sharpe'] for r in results])  # Must be > 1.0

Execution Quality & Slippage

Transaction Cost Analysis (TCA)

  • Slippage = actual fill - expected price
  • Exchange fees: 0.01% (maker) to 0.06% (taker)
  • Funding costs on perps (annualized 5-15%)
  • Track implementation shortfall per trade
  • Total cost = fees + slippage + market impact

Reducing Slippage

  • Use limit orders instead of market orders
  • Split large orders into smaller chunks (TWAP/VWAP)
  • Trade on high-liquidity venues (Binance, Bybit)
  • Avoid execution during low-volume hours
  • Compare fills across venues for best execution
# TWAP execution: split $50K order into 10 slices over 5 minutes
total_qty = 0.8  # BTC
slices = 10
for i in range(slices):
    exchange.create_limit_buy_order('BTC/USDT', total_qty / slices, best_ask)
    await asyncio.sleep(30)  # 30 seconds between slices

Portfolio Risk: Correlation & Leverage

  • BTC correlation — most altcoins correlate 0.6-0.9 with BTC during sell-offs
  • Diversification is limited in crypto vs traditional markets
  • Gross leverage = total position value / portfolio equity
  • Keep gross leverage under 3x for sustainable operation
  • Concentration limit — no single position > 20% of portfolio
  • Correlation spikes to ~1.0 during liquidation cascades

Gross Leverage

Sum of all |position values| / equity

($30K long + $20K short) / $25K = 2.0x

Net Exposure

Longs - Shorts / equity

($30K - $20K) / $25K = 0.4x net long

Value at Risk (VaR)

Max expected loss at 95% confidence over 24h. Crypto VaR is 3-5x higher than equities.

Stress Testing

Simulate -30% BTC crash, exchange outage, or funding rate spike. Ensure survival in all scenarios.

Regulatory & Security

API Key Security

  • IP whitelisting — restrict API keys to known IPs
  • No withdrawal permissions — trade-only API keys
  • Store keys in environment variables or secret managers
  • Rotate keys on a regular schedule (90 days)
  • Use subaccounts to isolate strategy risk

Regulatory Awareness

  • Jurisdiction matters: US, EU, Asia have different rules
  • Tax reporting: track cost basis for every trade
  • Futures may be restricted (US: no Binance futures)
  • MiCA (EU), MAS (Singapore), SEC/CFTC (US)
  • Keep detailed trade logs for compliance

Best Practices

1. Never risk more than 1-2% of portfolio on a single trade
2. Always use stop-losses — no exceptions, no mental stops
3. Paper trade every strategy for 2-4 weeks before going live
4. Account for fees, slippage, and funding in all backtests
5. Use API keys with trade-only permissions and IP whitelisting
6. Monitor correlation — five "diversified" alts may all be BTC bets
7. Keep gross leverage under 3x for long-term survival
8. Log every trade with entry reason, exit reason, and PnL
9. Use walk-forward testing, not just in-sample backtests
10. Build kill switches: auto-halt on max drawdown or API errors
